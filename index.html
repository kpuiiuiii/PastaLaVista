MADE FROM SCRATCH

Rigatoni.

Located in Chicago’s idyllic Gold Coast neighborhood, the former homes beckoned us to embark on renovations to reveal ornate details in a cozy and comforting environment. From its bones, it exuded the Italian spirit and begged us to welcome diners into the home to dine once again. Immediately, we knew this was to be Pasta La Vista Ristorante, our celebration of the Italian tradition served on the table and in the glass.

It is well known that Italians are deeply versed in the gioie della tavola, “the joys of the table.” The first thing people consider when they think of Italy is the warmth and magic created around the table, the place where life’s greatest experiences unfold, and lifelong relationships and memories are created and celebrated.

At Pasta La Vista, we welcome you into our home and invite you join us on a culinary journey as we use the dining table as our canvas while observing the fundamentals of Italian Cuisine – eat locally and eat seasonally. Allow us to revel in the art of cooking while you relish in the art of eating as we embrace the spirit of Italy by using the best local ingredients simply, seasonably, and sincerely.

HAND CRAFTED

Spaghetti.

Pasta La Vista has meticulously handcrafted pasta with the southern Sardinia style in mind. The multicultural clash in Sardinia over many centuries has influenced the combination of Spanish, African, and Arabic cuisine helping us develop our recipe.

We’re honored to have gained this knowledge, and many members of our team have spent countless hours attempting to perfect this technique. Keeping in mind that simplicity is the foundation of all cooking.

Our technique for pasta: Saffron is bloomed directly into the process of making the pasta, giving it the vibrant reddish-yellow color, and lovely floral aroma. That it is made fresh, with a shelf life between 12-24, and never dried. Each day the pasta is hand checked and tasted by our culinary staff. Whether it’s our, Agnolotti, Bucatini, or Pappardelle, cooking each style of pasta is timed to just the right bite or “al dente”.
